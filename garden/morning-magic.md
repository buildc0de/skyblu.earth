# Morning Magic Stack (AKA Amrit Vela [Container](/container-system/))

In Kundalini tradition, Amrit Vela is the ambrosial hours — the two and a half hours before sunrise when the mind is still porous, before the day's noise fills it in. I don't always make the full window. But the practice happens before anything else, or it doesn't happen.

This is what I do every morning. Eight elements, roughly ninety minutes, in the same order. I call it the Morning Magic Stack.

---

## The Stack

**How come?**
A brief reflection before anything else. Not journaling, not planning — just a single question turned inward: *how come?* It's a way of staying curious about my own life rather than just executing it. Sometimes the answer is obvious. Sometimes it surfaces something I didn't know was sitting there.

**Water**
750ml–1L on an empty stomach, usually with electrolytes and lime. Before the microdose, before the kriya, before anything. The body spent eight hours not drinking. This goes first.

**Microdose**
A small amount of psilocybin mushrooms — sub-perceptual, taken with intention. Not for experience, for edge. It sharpens the morning's focus and softens the inner critic enough to let the practices land deeper. I've been doing this long enough to know the difference a consistent practice makes.

**EFT**
Tapping. Emotional Freedom Technique. I use Brad Yates' recordings — specifically the money and abundance sequences. The mind knows what it wants; the nervous system often has a different opinion. Tapping is how I get them moving in the same direction. It doesn't feel elegant. It works anyway.

**DCA Readout**
My Declaration of Chief Aim, read aloud. Mine goes: *By July 15, 2026, I have a Prosperity System that steadily generates income, and $12,000 USD in my bank accounts. This is the foundation of a life of freedom, joyful service, and abundant prosperity.*

Reading it out loud every morning does something that silently holding the intention doesn't. The voice makes it real. The body hears it.

**Subagh Kriya**
A Kundalini yoga kriya for prosperity — five movements, each for three minutes, specific mudras and mantras. It's vigorous and a little strange if you've never done it. I've been practicing it long enough that it feels strange to skip. Something moves through the system when the kriya is done that nothing else quite replicates.

**Sit**
Stillness. Fifteen to thirty minutes of not moving. Not guided, not breath-focused — just the simple discipline of staying still. This one comes from Charles Haanel's *The Master Key System*, where Lesson 1's single exercise is exactly this: train the will by not letting the body fidget. The quality of quiet that comes from genuine stillness, as opposed to just closing your eyes, is different.

**Fascia Flow**
Fifteen minutes of slow, intentional movement — rolling, lengthening, releasing the connective tissue that's been compressed by sleep. Not a workout. A return to the body. The morning practices have been mostly inner; this is the bridge back out.

---

## Why This Order

Water grounds the body before anything else asks of it. The microdose needs to be taken early enough to settle before the kriya. EFT clears the morning emotional residue before the affirmation work — there's no point declaring a chief aim over top of unprocessed fear. The DCA lands cleaner after the tapping. Subagh Kriya generates energy that the sit then deepens. Fascia Flow closes the loop, so the body is ready to actually move through the world.

It took me a while to find this sequence. It's not arbitrary.

---

## What It Actually Does

The first thing I noticed: decisions are easier. The gap between "I should do that" and "I'm doing it" gets shorter. Not because the willpower increased — something in the orientation changed.

The second thing: my relationship with money stopped feeling like a fight. That shift didn't come from any single element. It came from consistent repetition of the whole stack, over weeks.

The third thing is harder to name. Something opens up early in the morning that closes by mid-day. Not productivity — presence. The stack keeps that window open longer.
