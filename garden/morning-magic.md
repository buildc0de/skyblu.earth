# Morning Magic Stack

I wake up at 5 AM most mornings — not because waking early is virtuous, but because this particular stretch of time before dawn is different from the rest. I call it the **Morning Magic Stack**. Eight elements, roughly 75 minutes. I also call it my Amrit Vela Container (more on Containers soon).

# Amrit Vela

In Kundalini tradition, Amrit Vela is the ambrosial hours — *amrit* being the nectar of immortality, *vela* the hour — the two and a half hours before sunrise when the mind is still spacious, before the day's noise fills it in.

# The Stack

## 'How come?' Question
As soon as I wake up, while still in bed, I ask myself a question: "How come there is so much abundance of money in my life?" It's an affirmation in the form of a question. The question bypasses defenses that a flat affirmation would trigger.

## Hydration
Once I get up, I prepare 1L of my custom electrolyte mix (recipe coming soon).

## Microdose
I sit down at my altar, light a candle and incense, and take 50-100mg of Golden Teacher. Not for experience — for edge. It sharpens my perceptions and stays with me all day.

## EFT
I recently discovered inner resistance to receiving more money. The mind knows what it wants; the emotional body has other opinions. Some from this life, some from further back. I added tapping or EFT (Emotional Freedom Techniques) to my day as an experiment to gently dissolve and resolve these pieces. I currently use [this sequence](https://youtu.be/qg11sMpCO0c) by Brad Yates.

## Chief Aim Readout
Then I read a written statement of my current primary life aim (Definite Chief Aim as inspired by *Think and Grow Rich* by Napoleon Hill). Reading it out loud every morning does something that silently holding the intention doesn't. The voice makes it real. The body hears it.

## Subagh Kriya
A Kundalini yoga kriya for prosperity — five movements, each for three minutes, specific mudras and mantras. It's vigorous and a little strange, if you've never done it. 

## Sit
Stillness. Fifteen minutes of not moving. Not guided, not breath-focused — just the simple discipline of staying still. While I've practiced different meditations over the years, this particular practice comes from *The Master Key System* by Charles Haanel, where Lesson 1's single exercise is exactly this: train the will by not letting the body fidget. 

## Fascia Flow
Fifteen minutes of a popular exercise sequence. The first two weeks released tightness my body held for 15+ years. Right now, I'm doing [this routine](https://youtu.be/WPhg9EZqSFE) by Jules Horn of Human Garage.
