# Classic Sauerkraut

Two ingredients. Weeks of patience. One of the most effective things you can do for your gut.

Sauerkraut is shredded cabbage fermented in its own brine. No vinegar, no heat, no shortcuts. Just salt, cabbage, time, and the bacteria already living on the leaves.

## What you need

- 1 medium head of green cabbage (~1 kg)
- 1–2% of its weight in non-iodized salt (sea salt, kosher salt)

That's it.

## How to make it

**1. Shred.** Remove the outer leaves and set one aside. Shred the cabbage thin — about 2–3 mm. A mandoline makes this fast.

**2. Salt and massage.** Weigh the shredded cabbage. Multiply by 0.02 for your salt amount. Add the salt and massage firmly for 5–10 minutes until the cabbage releases enough liquid to submerge itself.

**3. Pack the jar.** Press the cabbage tightly into a clean glass jar, pushing out air pockets. Fold the reserved outer leaf and use it to press everything below the brine. The cabbage must stay submerged — oxygen is the enemy here.

**4. Ferment.** Leave at room temperature (18–22°C is ideal), out of direct sunlight. Taste starting at day 5. It'll be mild and crunchy early, more sour and complex as weeks pass. Most people land somewhere between 1–4 weeks.

**5. Refrigerate.** Once it tastes right, seal the jar and move it to the fridge. It'll keep for months.

## Notes

- If you see white foam or bubbles in the first few days, that's normal — active fermentation.
- If you see fuzzy mold (pink, black, green), discard what's above the brine line, re-submerge, and carry on. The brine below is almost certainly fine.
- Iodized salt inhibits fermentation. Use sea salt or kosher.
- Warmer kitchens ferment faster. Cooler means slower, more complex flavor.

## Why bother

The live bacteria in raw sauerkraut are genuinely useful for digestion, immune function, and microbiome diversity — in ways that commercial pasteurized kraut is not. It's also cheap, almost impossible to fail at, and the fermentation process increases bioavailability of nutrients in the cabbage itself.

It's one of the highest leverage things you can make at home.
